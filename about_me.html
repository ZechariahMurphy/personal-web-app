About Me

"Telent is a persued interest. Anything that you're willing to practice, you can do."

- Bob Ross

I began my professional career as a history teacher. Every few months I’d get a brand new roster of kids. On the first day of each trimester I’d have the above quote clearly projected on the board. I had a quote on the board everyday, something motivating or introspective, but I’d use this quote on the first day for a number of reasons. First, I deeply admire Bob Ross. As a teacher I was inspired by his ability to entertain while educating. Also, one thing I’ve learned, and wanted to convey to my students, is how true this quote is. I wanted it to be the first thing they saw as they walked into my room and the first thing they thought about as they became my students. Believing you can achieve your goals is important, but that’s only a part of it. You have to pursue your goals, put plans into action, train and expand your knowledge in order to reach them. Everyday I told my students they were important, their dreams mattered, and they could be anything they worked hard enough to become. I taught that mentality everyday until I began to believe it myself, which ultimately gave me the confidence to leave teaching and pursue a lifelong interest in technology. Teaching had an enormous impact on my life, and I’m indebted to my students for their contribution, but as a history teacher I was always focused on the past and my passion is the future. Software development has been more fulfilling than I could have imagined. I’m willing to practice, and as I pursue my interest my talent grows. Thanks Bob.
